# Impeccable design context for Ken

## Design Context

### Users
Non-technical teams (ops, nonprofits, small orgs) who keep their shared knowledge in ordinary folders — Word docs, spreadsheets, PDFs — on Dropbox/OneDrive or Git. They open Ken in the morning to catch up ("what changed, what needs me?") and during the day to find, read, and edit documents. No developer vocabulary anywhere in the UI.

### Brand Personality
Calm, editorial, trustworthy — "a well-kept notebook, not a dashboard." Ken speaks in first-person plain language ("Ken is reading your folder…"), never jargon. The feel is warm paper and ink, not SaaS chrome.

### Aesthetic Direction
The design system is **Paper & Ink** and it is mandated by the owner's prototype (`docs/design/ken-prototype-v2.dc.html`, tokens in `docs/design/design-tokens.md` and `src/app.css`). Warm paper ground (#F6F4EF), soft surfaces, clay accent (#8A5A44) used sparingly, Source Serif 4 for editorial headings/lead prose, system sans for UI, IBM Plex Mono only for genuine file paths/terminal. Light by default (a dark variant exists as an option). Anti-references: analytics dashboards, hero-metric layouts, glassmorphism, neon-on-dark.

**Do not choose new fonts, hues, or a new direction — elevate craft within Paper & Ink.** (This overrides the skill's font-selection procedure: the type palette is fixed by the owner.)

### Design Principles
1. Editorial hierarchy over card grids — the day's digest reads like a morning briefing (serif lead), not a widget; not everything needs a card.
2. Rhythm through varied spacing — tight groupings, generous separations; one clear focal point per screen.
3. Accent is rare — clay for the one thing that matters now; amber only for "waiting on you"; everything else ink on paper.
4. Plain human copy — every word earns its place; empty states teach ("Ken writes you a morning digest — it'll appear here").
5. Quiet motion — one well-orchestrated entrance (staggered fade/rise, ease-out), state changes only; nothing bouncy.
